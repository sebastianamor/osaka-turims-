Ancient Era · 4th Century
Naniwa – Japan's First Capital
Osaka, then called Naniwa, served as the gateway between Japan and the Asian continent. Emperor Kotoku established the Naniwa Nagara-Toyosaki Palace here in 645 AD.
Naniwa Palace · Shitennoji Temple
城
Feudal Era · 1583
Toyotomi Hideyoshi Builds Osaka Castle
The warlord Toyotomi Hideyoshi chose Osaka as his base to unify Japan and began constructing the magnificent Osaka Castle in 1583.
Osaka Castle · Toyotomi Clan
商
Edo Period · 1600s–1800s
Tenka no Daidokoro – Japan's Kitchen
During the Edo period, Osaka became the nation's largest commercial hub. The Dojima Rice Exchange (1697) is considered the world's first futures market.
Dojima Rice Exchange · Merchant Culture
明
Meiji Era · 1868
Industrial Revolution & Modernization
The Meiji Restoration transformed Osaka into Japan's industrial capital, earning it the nickname 'the Manchester of the East.'
Textile Industry · National Exhibition 1903
現
Modern Era · 1945–Present
Rebirth as a Global City
After WWII, Osaka rebuilt and the 1970 World Expo put it on the global stage. Today it's Japan's second-largest economy attracting 10+ million visitors yearly.
Expo '70 · Universal Studios Japan · Osaka 2025
